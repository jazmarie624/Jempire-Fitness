⚠️ The Truth About "Shreds"

There are many products called "Shreds" online. Most are fat burners — caffeine, L-Carnitine, thermogenics. They may boost energy and slightly speed fat loss, but they do NOT build glutes. They can also suppress your appetite, which makes you accidentally eat too little protein — hurting muscle growth. If you want to try one, use it as a pre-workout on training days only. Never take it with coffee or your Alani.

💪 Creatine Monohydrate #1 MUST HAVE
The single most proven supplement for muscle growth in existence. It helps your muscles work harder every rep, recover faster, and grow bigger. Zero stimulants, no crash, no jitters. In the first week you might hold a tiny bit of water — totally normal, it goes away. Within 2–3 weeks you'll notice you can do more reps and lift heavier. That extra work = more glute growth.

What to buy: Any brand of Creatine Monohydrate works — the molecule is the same everywhere. Optimum Nutrition, Nutricost, and BulkSupplements are all solid and cheap (~$18–22 for 2 months on Amazon).
📌 5g daily — stir into water, milk, or your protein shake. Every single day including rest days.
🥤 Protein Powder ESSENTIAL
You cannot build glutes without enough protein. Most women eat 40–60g/day without realizing it. You need 120–130g. A protein shake fills that gap without cooking anything extra. Since you don't know your flavor yet, see the "Protein Picks" section below for exactly what to try first.

⚠️ Always read the label before buying — avoid any flavor with peanut butter or peanuts listed in ingredients. Stick to plain chocolate, vanilla, strawberry, or cookies & cream.

Your Weekly Plan

Glute growth is priority #1. Fat loss and toning follow automatically. Every lifting session is glute-focused. Active recovery days burn extra calories without fighting your muscle growth.

📅 Your Full Week — At a Glance
  • Monday — 🏋️ Lift: Heavy Glutes + Legs (Gym)
  • Tuesday — 🚴 Run OR Bike · 20–25 min easy pace · your choice that day
  • Wednesday — 🏋️ Lift: Glute Isolation + Core (Home)
  • Thursday — 😴 Rest day · full recovery · (Pilates optional if you have spare time & budget)
  • Friday — 🏋️ Lift: Power Day (Gym)
  • Saturday — 🚴 Run OR Bike · 20–25 min easy pace · your choice that day
  • Sunday — 😴 Full rest · do nothing · let your body rebuild and grow
🏃 Running vs Bike — your choice each active recovery day: Both are equally good for fat loss. Running outside burns slightly more calories and gives you a mental reset that the bike can't match — especially helpful when life is stressful. Bike is perfect for days you don't want to leave home. Either way: track TIME not miles. On the bike, ignore the mile counter — resistance changes everything. Just ride for 20–25 minutes. For running, set a timer on your phone and go. Easy conversational pace only. If you can't talk while running, slow down.
🧘 Pilates — spare time bonus only: Pilates won't grow your glutes significantly but it builds deep core stability and the mind-body connection that actually makes your glute exercises more effective. If you ever have spare time and budget, go. But your gym + home plan comes first, always. Pilates is never a replacement for a lifting day.
😌 On soreness: The first 2–3 weeks are the hardest. After that your body adapts and the soreness drops dramatically. Sunday and Thursday are your full recovery days — protect them.
📈 Most Important Rule — Progressive Overload: Every 1–2 weeks, add a little more weight or 1 more rep. Your glutes ONLY grow when you keep challenging them. Write your weights down after every single session. This is what separates women who transform from women who plateau.
Day 1 — Monday
🍑 Heavy Glutes + Legs · GYM
1
Barbell Hip Thrust
THE #1 glute builder. Back against a bench, bar across your hips, drive up and SQUEEZE for 1 second at the top. Ask gym staff to show you the setup on your first day — don't be shy.
4 × 10–12
2
Sumo Squat (Barbell or Dumbbells)
Wide stance, toes out 45°. Goes deeper into glutes and inner thighs than regular squats. Drive through your heels, keep chest up.
3 × 12
3
Cable Kickback
Ankle strap on cable machine. Kick one leg straight back and up slowly. Squeeze the glute hard at the top. No swinging — control every rep.
3 × 15 / side
4
Leg Press — Feet High on Platform
Placing feet high on the platform shifts all the work into your glutes and hamstrings instead of your quads. Go heavier than you think you can.
3 × 12
5
Band Lateral Walks
Band above knees, slight squat position, walk sideways 15 steps each direction. Creates the rounded "shelf" look from behind. Feel the burn in the outer glute.
3 × 15 each way
Day 2 — Wednesday
🔥 Glute Isolation + Core · HOME
1
Glute Bridge with Band
Lie on your back, band above knees, feet flat on floor. Drive hips up and hold 2 seconds at top. Feel it in your GLUTES — if you feel it in your lower back, move your feet closer to your body.
4 × 20
2
Single-Leg Deadlift (10lb Weights)
Stand on one leg, hinge forward with the weights hanging down, feel the stretch in your glute and hamstring, come back up squeezing hard. Amazing for the lower glute shape.
3 × 10 / leg
3
Donkey Kick with Band
On all fours, band above knees, kick one leg straight back and up. Squeeze at the top. Keep core tight — don't arch your lower back.
3 × 15 / side
4
Fire Hydrant with Band
Still on all fours, lift one knee out to the side. Works the outer glute for that rounded shape. Slow and controlled — feel every rep.
3 × 15 / side
5
Dead Bug (Core — Belly Safe)
Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor without touching, bring back up. Works your core deeply with zero pressure on your belly skin.
3 × 10 / side
Day 3 — Friday
💎 Power Day · GYM
1
Romanian Deadlift
Best exercise for the lower glute and that hamstring curve. Keep bar close to your legs, hinge at hips, feel the stretch, drive up through heels. This builds the bottom curve of the glute.
4 × 10
2
Smith Machine Hip Thrust
Same as Day 1 but more stable — the Smith Machine holds the bar steady so you can fully focus on squeezing. Go heavier than Monday.
4 × 10
3
Bulgarian Split Squat
Rear foot on bench, front foot forward, lower down slowly. Hardest exercise here — start bodyweight only. Incredible for glutes and thighs together.
3 × 8 / leg
4
Abductor Machine
Push knees apart against resistance. Shapes the outer glute — that look from behind. Go heavy. You'll feel it immediately in the side of your glute.
4 × 15
5
Walking Lunges
Long stride forward, torso upright, feel it in your glutes not your knees. Add your 10lb weights once bodyweight feels easy.
3 × 10 / leg

Food Weighing — Do You Need To?

You've seen people on Instagram weighing every gram of food. Here's the honest truth about whether you actually need to do that — and if so, exactly how it works.

Short answer for where you are right now: You do NOT need to weigh your food to start. Following the portion guidelines in this plan by eye is enough to get going and see real results. Weighing food is a more advanced tool — think of it as level 2. You can add it later once you've built the habit of eating right first.
⚖️ Should YOU Weigh Your Food?
Here's when weighing food actually makes sense and when it doesn't:

You DON'T need to weigh food if: You're just starting out, you're already making better food choices, and you're logging in MyFitnessPal using the cup/piece measurements already in the app. This is plenty for your first 8–12 weeks.

Weighing DOES help when: You've been consistent for 6–8 weeks and your progress has slowed or stalled. At that point, eyeballing portions can drift — your "6oz of steak" has become 9oz without you realizing it. A food scale brings it back to accuracy.
🔢 How Food Weighing Actually Works
  • Buy a digital food scale: About $10–15 on Amazon or Walmart. It sits on your counter and measures in grams and ounces. You place your plate or bowl on it, press "tare" (zero it out), then add your food.
  • Weigh protein raw, not cooked: Meat loses water when cooked so it weighs less after cooking. Always weigh your steak, chicken, shrimp, or ground beef BEFORE cooking for accurate numbers. 6oz raw = your target.
  • Weigh rice and pasta cooked: These absorb water when cooked so they weigh MORE after cooking. Weigh them after cooking. ½ cup cooked rice = about 100g.
  • Don't weigh vegetables: Broccoli, asparagus, carrots, cucumbers — don't stress about these. Eat as much as you want. They're too low in calories to matter.
  • Don't weigh fruit: Same — a banana is a banana, an apple is an apple. No need.
  • Use the tare button constantly: Tare means "zero out." Place your plate, press tare, add food, read the weight, press tare again, add the next food. You can weigh everything on one plate without mixing up numbers.
📏 Your Visual Portion Guide — No Scale Needed
Until you're ready to weigh, use your hand as a guide. This works surprisingly well:

  • Protein (steak, chicken, shrimp, salmon): The size and thickness of your palm = about 4–6oz. One palm per meal.
  • Rice or pasta: One cupped hand = about ½ cup cooked. One cupped hand per meal.
  • Sweet potato: About the size of your fist = one medium sweet potato.
  • Avocado: ½ avocado per serving — easy to measure visually.
  • Butter or olive oil for cooking: About 1 tablespoon = the tip of your thumb.
  • Shredded cheese: A small handful = about ¼ cup.
  • Vegetables: Fill the rest of your plate — no measuring needed.
🥑 Avocado, Shrimp + Pita — Your New Foods Explained
🥑 Avocado: Packed with healthy fats that support hormone production — which directly affects where your body stores fat and how your glutes grow. Also incredibly filling, which helps with the calorie deficit. Portion = ½ avocado per serving.

🍤 Shrimp: One of the leanest, highest-protein foods that exists — 6oz has about 36–38g of protein with very little fat or carbs. It cooks in 4 minutes. Season with sazon, lemon pepper, or adobo in butter.

🫓 Pita Bread: A great carb option for your plan. Better than regular bread because it's denser, more filling, and holds together well. One whole pita = about 160–170 calories and 30g of carbs — perfect as a pre-workout energy source or a lunch wrap.

🐟 Canned Tuna + Canned Chicken: Two of the most underrated high-protein convenience foods. Already cooked, zero prep, cheap. 1 can tuna = about 25–30g protein for under 150 calories. Mix with a tiny bit of mayo, corn, garlic powder — done in 2 minutes.

🌶️ Red Peppers + Green Peppers: Add these to ground beef, chicken, or shrimp whenever you want. They add color, crunch, vitamins, and flavor — and they're so low in calories (about 25 calories per pepper) that they don't even count toward your totals.
🥗 Caesar, Ranch + Mayo — The Real Rules
You do NOT have to cut these out. You just have to measure them.

Caesar Dressing: 2 tablespoons = about 150–160 calories and totally fine on this plan. Pour it by eye and you'll easily use 4–6 tablespoons without realizing it — that's 300–450 extra calories from dressing alone.

Ranch Dressing: Same rule — 2 tablespoons measured, not poured. Great on salads, as a dip for carrots and cucumbers, or drizzled on chicken.

Mayonnaise: 1 teaspoon in your tuna or canned chicken mix is completely fine. The problem is when people use mayo as a spread and go way over without noticing.

The rule for all three: Measure it. Use a spoon. Never pour directly from the bottle. 2 tablespoons of Caesar = delicious and on plan. Pouring from the bottle = 450 extra calories you didn't count.
📌 Buy a set of measuring spoons ($3 at Walmart) and keep them on your counter. This one habit saves you hundreds of hidden calories every single week.
